2. ShipFast

$199 one-time · Next.js 14 · MongoDB · Mailgun · Stripe · NextAuth

It focuses on speed-to-market and includes a set of pre-built landing page components, Stripe integration, and MongoDB for data storage. Its popularity means more tutorials, community support, and third-party guides are available.

Strengths

  • ✓ Large, active community and Discord
  • ✓ Well-tested with thousands of users
  • ✓ Good landing page components out of the box
  • ✓ Lifetime updates included

Trade-offs

  • - $199 price point -- 2.5x the cost of LaunchFast
  • - Still on Next.js 14 (two major versions behind)
  • - No built-in AI integration
  • - MongoDB instead of a relational database

3. Makerkit

$249 one-time (1 yr updates) · Next.js 15 · Supabase · Resend · Stripe / Lemon Squeezy

Makerkit stands out with its robust multi-tenancy support, making it a strong choice if you are building a B2B SaaS that requires team workspaces and organization management. It is tightly integrated with Supabase and offers a polished admin dashboard built with Shadcn UI. The $249 price includes one year of updates.

Strengths

  • ✓ Best multi-tenancy and team management
  • ✓ Supports both Stripe and Lemon Squeezy
  • ✓ Well-documented with guides and tutorials
  • ✓ Clean Shadcn UI-based dashboard

Trade-offs

  • - $249 with only 1 year of updates
  • - Supabase lock-in (not easy to switch DBs)
  • - No AI integration included
  • - Heavier setup process for new developers

4. Nextbase

$149 one-time · Next.js 15 · Supabase · Resend · Stripe · Optional AI add-on

At $149, it sits in the mid-range of pricing and offers good value for Supabase-based projects.

Strengths

  • ✓ Multi-tenancy built in
  • ✓ Lifetime updates at a reasonable price
  • ✓ Optional AI module available

Trade-offs

  • - AI is an add-on, not included by default
  • - Supabase lock-in
  • - Smaller community than ShipFast
  • - Less landing page variety out of the box

5. SaasterKit

$129 one-time · Next.js 14 · Prisma + PostgreSQL · Nodemailer · Stripe · NextAuth

  • - Smaller feature set overall

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a SaaS starter kit?

A SaaS starter kit is a pre-built codebase that includes common SaaS features like authentication, payment processing, database integration, email systems, and a dashboard UI. Instead of building these from scratch, developers use starter kits to skip months of boilerplate work and launch their product faster. Most are built on modern frameworks like Next.js and include integrations with services like Stripe, Resend, and various auth providers.

How much time does a SaaS starter kit save?

A good SaaS starter kit saves 4-8 weeks of development time by providing pre-built authentication, payment integration, database schema, email templates, and dashboard UI. Instead of wiring up Stripe webhooks, configuring OAuth providers, and building a billing portal from scratch, you get production-ready code on day one. The actual time saved depends on your experience level and how many features the kit includes.

Should I build my SaaS from scratch or use a starter kit?

Use a starter kit if you want to validate your idea quickly and focus on your unique product features rather than infrastructure. Build from scratch if you have very specific architectural requirements, need a non-standard tech stack, or want complete control over every line of code. For most indie developers and small teams, a starter kit at $79-$299 pays for itself within the first week of saved development time.
