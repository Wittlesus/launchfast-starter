The Story

I'm a full-stack developer who's built SaaS products for the past 6 years. Every time I started a new project, I'd spend 2-3 weeks setting up the same infrastructure: NextAuth for authentication, Stripe for payments, Prisma for the database, middleware for route protection.

By the third project, I was frustrated. Why was I rebuilding OAuth flows from scratch? Why was I re-implementing Stripe webhook handlers? Why was I debugging the same middleware edge cases?

So I built LaunchFast. Not as a product to sell (at first), but as my own starter template. I used it to ship two SaaS products in 2025. Both went from idea to paying customers in under two weeks because I didn't waste time on infrastructure.

Other developers asked for the code. Instead of copy-pasting it into DMs, I cleaned it up, documented it properly, and put it on GitHub. The positive response convinced me to turn it into a product.

LaunchFast is code I use myself. It's not built by a faceless agency or a VC-backed startup trying to maximize revenue. It's built by someone who ships products and got tired of the setup grind.
